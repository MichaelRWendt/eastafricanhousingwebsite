"What are 'Housing stabilization services'?"

Housing Transition Services

Housing Transition Services helps a person plan for, find, and move into housing and may include:
1. Helping the person think about preferred housing
2. Helping with the housing search and application process
3. Developing a budget
4. Understanding a lease

Housing Sustaining Services

Housing Sustaining Services help a person keep their housing after they have moved in and may include:
1. Education on tenant-landlord rights and responsibilities
2. Coaching to develop relationships with property managers and neighbors
3. Training on how to be a good tenant; lease compliance
4. Problem-solving to maintain housing stability

Housing Consultation Services

Housing Consultation Services help a person who does not have Medical Assistance (MA) case management develop a person-centered plan that addresses their needs, wants, and goals for living in the community and may include:
1. Developing a housing focused person-centered plan based on the person's needs, wants, and goals for housing
2. Helping a person make an informed choice in their housing transition or sustaining services provider
3. Offering resources related to non-housing goals
4. Coordinating with other service providers already working with the person

Eligibility

In order to qualify for our services, you must be currently enrolled in Minnesota's Medical Assistance program.
You can apply for Medical Assistance at your county or tribal human services office,
click here for the full Minnesota list of county health care offices & Tribal health care offices.

To be eligible, you must be 18 years or older--we do not specialize in housing minors.
You must have a disability or disabling condition and you must requires assistance with
at least one of the following areas due to a disability or disabling condition:
◘ Communication ◘ Mobility ◘ Decision making ◘ Managing challenging behaviors

Payment through Medicaid
